Password Best Practices

1

Use at least 12-16 characters

Length is the single most important factor in password entropy. Each additional character increases combinations exponentially.

2

Mix Character Classes

Ensure your password blends uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols to enlarge the key space (range pool) of potential crack combinations.

3

Avoid Common Patterns

Do not use sequential letters or numbers (e.g. abcde, 12345) or adjacent keyboard rows (e.g. qwerty), as dictionary attacks guess these first.

4

Never Reuse Passwords

If a service experiences a breach, attackers will use credential stuffing to test those passwords across other websites. Use unique passwords for every site.
